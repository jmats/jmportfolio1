    • Hi there! I'm Julia.
    _

    I'm a self-taught designer based in Brooklyn, New York. My design journey (from graphic, to web, to UX, then to Product) was meandering but delightful and full of surprises. I started out in business operations management because I enjoyed the challenge of ensuring that multiple functions of a company integrated seamlessly together - in short, cross-functional leadership and ownership.

    While I excelled at business operations, I realized there was something missing. I began exploring possibilities of developing skillsets related to the design world, and it was during this time that I was given a project at NYU that required extensive graphic and information design knowledge. So, with the full support of my team (thanks Helen & Jeremy!), I began taking design classes at NYU along with supplemental online courses.

    This project opened the door to my design journey, which led me to web design, then User Experience Design. UxD caught my attention because it was the perfect synethesis of functionality and design (I must be honest: the psychology part intrigued me as well). It makes me itch whenever I encounter inefficient systems or badly designed products, so imagine my delight when I discovered there was an entire field dedicated to Improving Usability! I started my UX apprenticeship with Bloc the same time I began my start-up adventure at Caper. There, I managed day-to-day operations and oversaw product design and management.

    My most recent focus is learning about design systems - how to build one, what makes it good, and who can I learn from (if you're interested, here's a cool read from Airbnb to start!). I'll always be passionate about building systems and processes with efficiency and excellence in mind. There's so much more to learn and discover, and I can't wait to see what's in store. Thanks for stopping by, and do say hi if you feel so inclined!
